How to Choose the Right Arch Support for Your Foot Type

June 17, 2026 by Currex Blog
arch support foot type wet test high arch low arch flat feet insole guide CURREX

Choosing the right arch support starts with knowing your foot type. The wrong arch support can be uncomfortable or even counterproductive, but the right one can make a world of difference in comfort, performance, and injury prevention.

In this guide, we'll show you how to identify your arch type and match it with the perfect insole.

The Simple Wet Test

You can identify your arch type at home in 30 seconds with the wet test:

  1. Wet your foot — Step into a shallow tray of water or wet your foot in the shower
  2. Step onto a surface — Step onto a piece of paper, a brown paper bag, or a dark flat surface
  3. Look at your footprint — Compare it to the images below

What Your Footprint Says

You see most of your foot with a wide middleLow Arch / Flat FeetFeet tend to roll inward (overpronation), more flexibleYou see about half of your archMedium / Neutral ArchNormal arch, neutral stride, most common foot typeYou see mostly your heel and forefoot with a narrow middleHigh ArchFeet tend to roll outward (supination), less flexibleVery little contact in the middle, almost two separate printsVery High ArchSignificant supination, poor shock absorption

Arch Types Explained

Low Arch / Flat Feet

What happens: The arch collapses toward the ground, causing the foot to roll inward (overpronation). This can lead to shin splints, plantar fasciitis, and knee pain.

What you need: A supportive insole with firm arch support to help control overpronation. Look for insoles with a Low Arch (Red) profile.

Medium / Neutral Arch

What happens: The foot functions normally with a balanced gait. You have moderate pronation and good shock absorption naturally.

What you need: A balanced insole with medium arch support that provides comfort without over-correcting. Look for insoles with a Medium Arch (Orange/Yellow) profile.

Best CURREX match:


High Arch

What happens: The foot has a pronounced arch that doesn't flatten enough during walking. This causes supination (rolling outward) and poor shock absorption.

What you need: An insole with good cushioning and a higher arch profile to fill the space and provide support. Look for insoles with a High Arch (Blue) profile.
